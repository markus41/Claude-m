# Extended Naming Patterns & Techniques

## Compound Name Patterns

### Noun + Noun
Combine two concrete nouns to create a vivid image.

| Pattern | Examples |
|---------|----------|
| Object + Place | Firehouse, Snowflake, Cloudflare |
| Animal + Object | HawkEye, FireFox, MailChimp |
| Nature + Tech | SunPower, WaveMaker, WindStream |
| Action + Object | DropBox, SnapChat, CrashPlan |

### Adjective + Noun
Pair a descriptor with a noun for instant meaning.

| Pattern | Examples |
|---------|----------|
| Color + Noun | RedHat, Greenlight, BlueHost |
| Size + Noun | BigCommerce, TinyURL, MegaPath |
| Quality + Noun | BrightEdge, SwiftKey, ClearScore |
| Speed + Noun | FastSpring, QuickBooks, RapidAPI |

### Verb + Noun
Action-oriented names that convey what the product does.

| Pattern | Examples |
|---------|----------|
| Action + Object | SurveyMonkey, SendGrid, ParseHub |
| Movement + Target | LaunchDarkly, PushOver, FetchRewards |

## Portmanteau Techniques

### Front + Back Blend
Take the front of one word and the back of another.

- **Pinterest** = Pin + Interest
- **Groupon** = Group + Coupon
- **Brandfolder** = Brand + Folder
- **Palantir** = Pala(ce) + (Ma)ntir (seeing stone)

### Overlap Blend
Words share overlapping sounds or letters.

- **Instagram** = Instant + Telegram
- **Shopify** = Shop + (-ify suffix)
- **Bitly** = Bit + (-ly suffix)
- **Calendly** = Calendar + (-ly suffix)

### Truncation Blend
Shorten one or both words before combining.

- **Cisco** = San Fran(cisco)
- **Intel** = Intel(ligence)
- **Qualcomm** = Qual(ity) + Comm(unications)

## Invented Word Strategies

### Phonetic Construction
Build words from pleasant-sounding syllables.

**Strong consonant openings**: B, K, S, T, Z
**Soft vowel endings**: -a, -o, -i, -oo
**Rhythmic patterns**: Two syllables preferred (Hulu, Roku, Yelp)

**Examples:**
- Spotify (Sp + ot + ify)
- Zillow (Z + ill + ow)
- Venmo (Ven + mo)
- Trello (Tr + ell + o)

### Letter Substitution
Modify real words with letter swaps.

- **Lyft** ← Lift (y for i)
- **Tumblr** ← Tumbler (drop e)
- **Flickr** ← Flicker (drop e)
- **Dribbble** ← Dribble (extra b)

## Metaphor & Symbolism

### Nature Metaphors
Evoke natural qualities.

| Quality | Nature Word | Used By |
|---------|------------|---------|
| Growth | Sprout, Bloom, Seed | Sprout Social |
| Speed | Falcon, Cheetah, Swift | Swift (Apple) |
| Strength | Oak, Iron, Granite | Ironclad |

| Vision | Eagle, Hawk, Horizon | HawkEye |

Borrow from cultural stories.

- **Amazon** — Largest river, fierce warriors
- **Nike** — Greek goddess of victory
- **Oracle** — Ancient prophecy source
- **Palantir** — Tolkien's seeing stones
- **Atlas** — Titan holding the world

## Industry-Specific Patterns

### SaaS / B2B
- Tend toward compound or suffix names
- Professional but approachable
- Examples: Salesforce, HubSpot, Zendesk, Freshworks

### Consumer Apps
- Short, fun, memorable
- Often invented words
- Examples: TikTok, Snapchat, Venmo, Hulu

### FinTech
- Trust and stability signals
- Often compound with finance words
- Examples: Stripe, Plaid, Brex, Revolut

### Health / Wellness
- Soft sounds, positive associations
- Nature and vitality imagery
- Examples: Calm, Headspace, Noom, Hims

### AI / ML
- Technical but accessible
- Often reference intelligence or learning
- Examples: DeepMind, DataRobot, Weights & Biases

### Developer Tools
- Short, punchy, technical
- Often action-oriented
- Examples: Vercel, Netlify, Supabase, Railway

## Name Length Guidelines

| Length | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|--------|------|------|----------|
| 3-5 chars | Highly memorable, easy to type | Hard to find available domains | Consumer apps |
| 6-8 chars | Good balance of memorable and available | May lose some distinctiveness | Most businesses |
| 9-12 chars | More domain availability, more descriptive | Harder to type quickly | B2B, descriptive brands |
| 13+ chars | Very descriptive, domain-rich | Hard to remember, long URLs | SEO-focused or temporary |

## Naming Anti-Patterns

Avoid these common mistakes:

1. **Too similar to competitors** — Confusion and trademark risk
2. **Hard to spell from hearing** — Users can't find the website
3. **Negative meanings in other languages** — Research internationally
4. **Trending suffixes** — Overused patterns feel generic (avoid .ly fatigue)
5. **Numbers in names** — Confusing (is it "4" or "four"?)
6. **Hyphens in domains** — Look unprofessional, hard to communicate verbally
7. **Overly narrow names** — "BostonPizzaDelivery" doesn't scale
8. **Inside jokes or obscure references** — Meaningless to most customers
